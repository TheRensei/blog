# About
I have been playing Call of Cthulhu since around Match 2024. It was the first TTRPG I played as a Game Master, and while I'm still new to this kind of thing I am having a lot of fun playing with a couple of my friend groups. Most of you probably know the pain of trying to organise regular games for an ongoing campaign, and that is the main reason we ended up focusing on one-shots or episodic way of playing. 
There is a lot of material available online (like [scenario seeds](https://www.dholeshouse.org/Utilities/SeedsOfDoom), [one-page scenarios](https://reckoningofthedead.wordpress.com/one-page-scenarios/), or a ton of stuff from [miskatonic repository](https://www.drivethrurpg.com/en/publisher/2/Chaosium/category/29274/Miskatonic-Repository?page=1&sort=4a)) and all of it is amazing!

Recently though I've started to run short games at work for friends using a small idea and improvising everything else on the go. I start with having an idea for the premise of the scenario, describe it to my players - then we quickly chat about what kind of occupation they'd like to have. We use [Dhole's House Character Library](https://www.dholeshouse.org/CharacterLibrary/CoC7edInvestigators) (or [AntagonistsRPG CoC Generator](https://www.antagonistsrpg.com/coc_pregen/?) )to then get some premades. Before we start the game I like to discuss quickly about some backstory for their characters, do they know each other, what could be their reason for participating in this specific scenario etc etc. The rest of out game is pure improvisation on their and my part.

Well, to my surprise they are having quite a lot of fun! And as per their recommendation I've decided to start writing one-shots based on the successful games we've had. I'm not a writer, not I pretend to be one, I don't think I'm a good Keeper either, but I hope that you'll find something even a lil bit useful here!
